### 1. Target Audience
**Question:** Who is the core target audience? Are we building this primarily for developers/engineers, creative writers, academic researchers, or a specific niche that feels underserved?
**Your Answer:**
The target audience is for experience PKM users who are looking for a more integrated and robust multi-agent experience.  It combines the best features of common PKM editors like Obsidian, Logseq, and Notion, but with a more powerful and opinionated AI-first experience built right in. 

### 2. The "Superpower" (Primary Differentiator)
**Question:** Apps like Obsidian focus on graph-linking, Notion focuses on block-based databases, and VS Code focuses on extensibility. What is the one thing Glade will do better than anyone else? Is it the WYSIWYG editing, the speed of Tauri, or the upcoming Agentic AI?
**Your Answer:**
Glade has two major capabilities that I found missing in other knowledge management system.
1. A truly opinionated integrated multi-agent system that works seamlessly with the knowledge management system. It should be able to help users to organize their knowledge in a way that makes sense to them, and also to help them to create new knowledge in a way that is easy for them to understand and use.


2. PKM tools like obsidian tend to treat vaults as all or nothing and they are disconnected.  Glade allows for users to create 'contained' vaults very similar to Obsidian but have them also set in the context of collections of vaults and have first class constructs on how vaults can be interconnected and leveraged. For instance being able to pull content from one connected vault into a another fault or using semantic searching across multiple vaults. This is really useful for people who are working on multiple projects or who are working with different teams.

### 3. Core Philosophy
**Question:** What is the core philosophy regarding user data? Are we strictly local-first and privacy-focused? Are we treating vaults simply as local Git repositories?
**Your Answer:**
The core philosophy is that the vault is strictly local and personal.  We obviously use cloud based inference but the user if they so choose can also connect there own inference engine. 

### 4. Agentic AI Integration
**Question:** How central is the "Agentic AI Core" to the identity of the product? Is the AI an optional plugin that people can turn on for help, or is Glade fundamentally an "AI-native" knowledge base that proactively helps you organize and write?
**Your Answer:**
Glade is fundamentally an "AI-native" knowledge base that proactively helps you organize and write.  The users may choose to turn off AI features but we are not building a replacement for the amazing things that Obsidian and Notion do well.  Instead, we are building an AI-native capabilities.  There is also a trade-off between completely freeform AI integration (hallucinations, document sprawl and quickly creating a vault that is unmanageable) and a highly structured approach that prevents users from straying too far from the structure ()
